Races

Human

  • Ability Scores: You have a +1 bonus to three different ability scores of your choice. Humans are versatile and individuals can vary greatly from each other.
  • Size: You are a Medium creature. The average height and build of a Human varies between regions, but they are typically between five and six feet tall and weigh between 100 and 160 pounds.
  • Movement: You have a 30 foot land speed.
  • Languages: You can speak, read, and write Common as well as one of the following languages: Vrillian, Serelic, or Tyrili.
  • Perseverance: When you roll a 1 on an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw, you can reroll the die. You must take the new result. Grim prospects only serve as further encouragement for Humans to overcome their obstacles.
  • Versatility: You have proficiency in two skills of your choice. Human cultures can be quite different, and so are the skills they may learn growing up.

Eldaari

  • Ability Scores: You have a +2 bonus to Intelligence and a +1 bonus to Dexterity. The Eldaari are known for their scholarly achievements, and their slender frames make them naturally agile.
  • Size: You are a Medium creature. Few Eldaari are shorter than six feet tall; the average Eldaari height is six feet four inches. They are also relatively thin, with an average weight of 140 pounds for an Eldaari of average height.
  • Movement: You have a 30 foot land speed.
  • Languages: You can speak, read, and write Common and Eldaarish.
  • Fundamental Magic: You know one cantrip of your choice from the Wizard spell list. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for it. All Eldaari, even if they do not study it further, are introduced to magic at a young age.
  • Arcane Insight: You can cast Detect Thoughts once per day. When you reach 3rd level, you can also cast Detect Magic once per day. When you reach 5th level, you can also cast See Invisbility once per day. You must finish a long rest to cast these spells once again with this trait. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for these spells. Eldaari perception is enhanced by their innate affinity for magic.
  • Arcane Poise: You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed, and magic can't put you to sleep. Generations of exposure to the arcane have made Eldaari minds less vulnerable to magical manipulation.

Feldani

  • Ability Scores: You have a +2 bonus to Dexterity and a +1 bonus to Constitution. Both nimble and tough, Feldani are well-suited for life in the wilderness.
  • Size: You are a Medium creature. Feldani are both short and slim, with most standing slightly over 5 feet tall, and weighing 100 pounds or less.
  • Movement: You have a 35 foot land speed. Feldani are not only known for their agility, but also their speed.
  • Languages: You can speak, read, and write Common and Feldan.
  • Darkvision: You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray. Hunting and foraging in dark forests have made Feldani used to low-light conditions.
  • Guerilla Fighter: If you surprise a creature and hit it with an attack on your first turn in combat, the attack deals an extra 2d6 damage to it. You can use this trait only once per combat. The Feldani military is known for its usage of guerilla warfare and highly skilled assassins.
  • Blades in the Grass: You can attempt to hide even when you are only lightly obscured by foliage, heavy rain, falling snow, mist, and other natural phenomena. The use of natural camouflage is second nature for the Feldani.

Dwerger

  • Ability Scores: You have a +2 bonus to Constitution and a +1 bonus to Wisdom. A hostile living environment has made Dwerger resilient in both mind and body.
  • Size: You are a Medium creature. Dwerger are generally wide-framed and muscular, but short. Most are a bit under 5 feet tall and roughly 140 pounds.
  • Movement: You have a 25 foot land speed. The short stature of the Dwerger makes quick movement difficult.
  • Languages: You can speak, read, and write Common and Runic.
  • Superior Darkvision: You can see in dim light within 120 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray. Lighting was a luxury in the underground ancestral homes of the Dwerger.
  • Steady Movement: Your speed is not reduced by wearing heavy armor. Though they do not move quickly, Dwerger are not easily encumbered by either.
  • Stable Stance: You count as one size larger when determining if another creature is able to grapple or shove you. Due to their concentrated distribution of weight, Dwerger are extremely stable on their feet
  • Hardy Physique: Your hit point maximum increases by 1, and it increases by 1 every time you gain a level. Dwerger are a hardy people, and can survive through more injury than most.
  • Heart of the Dwerger: You have resistance against cold damage. Nordingard's frozen landscape has toughened Dwerger against the cold.

Ul'Raathi

  • Ability Scores: You have a +2 bonus to Strength and a +1 bonus to Constitution. The physical might of the Ul'Raathi has contributed greatly to their reputation as fearsome warriors.
  • Size: You are a Medium creature. Tall and imposing, the average Ul'Raathi adult is six feet tall and weighs 190 pounds.
  • Movement: You have a 30 foot land speed.
  • Languages: You can speak, read, and write Common, Ul'Raathan, and one extra language of your choice. The Ul'Raathi are a traditionally seafaring people, and both merchants and mercenaries alike learn the languages of their clients in order to ply their trade more effectively.
  • Unyielding: When you are reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright; you can drop to 1 hit point instead. You can't use this feature again until you finish a long rest. Soldiers often tell tales of Ul'Raathi mercenaries continuing to fight through seemingly fatal wounds.
  • Fearless: You have advantage on saving throws against being frightened. It is extremely difficult to intimidate an Ul'Raathi, though most dare not try in the first place.
  • Perfect Blow: When you score a critical hit with a melee weapon attack, you can roll one of the weapon's damage dice one additional time and add it to the extra damage of the critical hit. Ul'Raathi warriors are far from simple-minded brutes, and spend much time training to be precise in their strikes.
  • Tireless Advance: As a bonus action, you can move up to your movement speed toward a hostile creature you can see or hear. You must end this move closer to the enemy than you started. Overwhelming force is a mainstay of Ul'Raathi tactics, in part due to the incredible speed at which they can charge their enemies.

Saurian

  • Ability Scores: You have a +2 bonus to Dexterity and a +1 bonus to Wisdom. Saurians are quite dexterous in spite of their reptilian features, and are often very spritual individuals.
  • Size: You are a Medium creature. Saurians are tall and slender, with an average height of about 6 feet, but their dense muscle makes them surprisingly heavy, weighing on average 210 pounds.
  • Movement: You have a 30 foot land speed and a 30 foot swim speed. Saurians possess webbed feet and powerful legs that make swimming as natural as walking for them.
  • Languages: You can speak, read, and write Common and Saurian.
  • Darkvision: You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray. Ancient Saurians would typically hunt at night in order to more easily surprise their prey.
  • Hold Breath: You can hold your breath for up to 15 minutes at a time. Saurians often gather underwater plants for use as food or medicine, or sometimes religious rituals.
  • Natural Armor: When you aren't wearing armor, your AC is 13 + your Dexterity modifier. You can use your natural armor to determine your AC if the armor you wear would leave you with a lower AC. A shield's benefits apply as normal while you use your natural armor. Thick scales protect Saurians from smaller threats, though armor still sees use in warfare.
  • Tail Attack: Your tail is a natural weapon, which you can use to make unarmed strikes. If you hit with it, you deal bludgeoning damage equal to 1d6 + your Strength modifier, instead of the damage normal for an unarmed strike. Saurians have extremely powerful tails that can be utilized to deadly effect.
  • Wetland Camouflage: You have advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks to hide in water or muddy terrain. Hiding just underneath the water is a common tactic used by Saurian hunters and soldiers alike.

Magnian

  • Ability Scores: You have a +2 bonus to Strength and a +1 bonus to Wisdom. Magians are well-known for their immense strength as well as their patient demeanor.
  • Size: You are a Medium creature. Magnians are not particularly short or tall, with an average height of 5 feet 6 inches, but are extremely heavy, weighing on average upwards of 250 pounds.
  • Movement: You have a 30 foot land speed.
  • Languages: You can speak, read, and write Common and Magnar.
  • Powerful Build: You count as one size larger when determining your carrying capacity and the weight you can push, drag, or lift. You also count as one size larger when determining if you are able to grapple or shove another creature. The sheer weight and strength of Magnians is extraordinary for their size.
  • Arm Slam: Your arms are natural weapons, which you can use to make unarmed strikes. If you hit with it, you deal bludgeoning damage equal to 1d6 + your Strength modifier, instead of the damage normal for an unarmed strike. Magnians can swing their arms like cudgels, inflicting much more damage than a typical untrained punch.
  • Long-Limbed: When you make a melee attack on your turn, your reach for it is 5 feet greater than normal. Magnians have especially long arms that allow them to lunge further when attacking.
  • Tackle: When you take the Dash action and move at least 20 feet, you may take a Shove action at the end of your movement. A charging Magnian can use their own mass a weapon.

Feyling

  • Ability Scores: You have a +2 bonus to Charisma and a +1 bonus to Dexterity. Charming and flighty, Feyling are quick in both speech and movement.
  • Size: You are a Small creature. Feylings are small in stature and very light, with an average height of just a little over 3 feet and weigh 30 pounds on average.
  • Movement: You have a 25 foot land speed.
  • Languages: You can speak, read, and write Common and Sylvan.
  • Feyling Resistance: You have advantage on all Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma saving throws against magic, and magic can't put you to sleep. The minds of Feylings are not easily affected by magic.
  • Planar Wings: When you reach 3rd level, you can also cast the Feather Fall spell once per day. When you reach 5th level, you can also cast Fly once per day. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells. The move to the material plane has impaired many Feyling abilities, but they can still materialize their wings for short periods of time.
  • Nature's Friend: Through sounds and gestures, you can communicate simple ideas with Small or smaller beasts. They can understand the meaning of your words, though you have no special ability to understand them in return. Feylings feel at home in nature, and often make friends with the animals that inhabit it.

Gremlux

  • Ability Scores: You have a +2 bonus to Wisdom and a +1 bonus to Constitution. The dangers of living in the Deep Realms have made Gremlux both patient and tough.
  • Size: You are a Small creature. Gremlux are built for life underground, with an average height just under 4 feet tall and weighing about 70 pounds on average.
  • Movement: You have a 25 foot land speed.
  • Languages: You can speak, read, and write Common and one extra language of your choice.
  • Superior Darkvision: You can see in dim light within 120 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray. Gremlux originated from the Deep Realms and had nearly no access to light.
  • Telepathic Message: You can speak telepathically to any creature within 30 feet of you. The creature understands you only if the two of you share a language. You can speak telepathically in this way to one creature at a time. Though limited, Gremlux possess some psionic ability.
  • Psionic Shielding: You have resistance against psychic damage. Gremlux had to develop natural defenses against psionic attacks to survive the threats that dwell deeper underground.
  • Breath Control: You can hold your breath indefinitely while you're not incapacitated. When navigating underground caves, Gremlux would often have to hold their breath to avoid toxic gases.

Tatsu

  • Ability Scores: You have a +2 bonus to Charisma and a +1 bonus to Wisdom. Natural rulers, the Tatsu are eloquent speakers and prudent actors.
  • Size: You are a Medium creature. Tatsu are tall and graceful, averaging six feet tall with an average weight of 150 pounds.
  • Movement: You have a 30 foot land speed.
  • Languages: You can speak, read, and write Common and Daitien.
  • Dragon Breath: You can use your action to exhale destructive energy. Your Imperial House determines the damage type of the exhalation. When you use your breath weapon, each creature in a 15 foot cone must make a saving throw, the type of which is determined by your breath type. The DC for this saving throw equals 8 + your Constitution modifier + your proficiency bonus. A creature takes 2d6 damage on a failed save, and half as much damage on a successful one. The damage increases to 3d6 at 6th level, 4d6 at 11th level, and 5d6 at 16th level. After you use your breath weapon, you can't use it again until you complete a short or long rest. The draconic ancestry of the four Imperial Houses has granted them the ability to unleash dragon's breath.

    Onyx Poison (Con Save)
  • Draconic Resistance: You have resistance to the damage type associated with your Imperial House. Tatsu are naturally resistant to the type of dragon breath they are able to expel.
  • Imperial Tongue: When you reach 3rd level, you can cast the Charm Person spell once per day with this trait. When you reach 5th level, you can also cast the Enthrall spell once per day with this trait. You must finish a long rest to cast these spells once again with this trait. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells. Though already naturally charismatic, Tatsu also possess the inherent ability to magically charm others.

Tengu

  • Ability Scores: You have a +2 bonus to Strength and a +1 bonus to Dexterity. Like birds of prey, Tengu are swift and powerful warriors.
  • Size: You are a Medium creature. Tengu are fairly short and light. Most are a little over 5 feet tall and weigh on average about 90 to 100 pounds.
  • Movement: You have a 30 foot land speed.
  • Languages: You can speak, read, and write Common and Daitien.
  • Glide: You have a flying speed of 30 feet, but you land at the end of your turn. You take falling damage as normal if you fall from a high enough height. To use this speed, you can't be wearing medium or heavy armor. Tengu have wings on their back that allow for short bouts of flight, but their humanoid frames are not ideal for anything more.
  • Warrior Spirit: When you are reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright; you can drop to 1 hit point instead. You can't use this feature again until you finish a long rest. Tengu are known to continue standing after grievous injuries through sheer force of will.
  • Eagle Eyes: You have proficiency in the Perception skill. Keen vision is another trait that Tengu share with flying raptors.
  • Talons: Your talons are natural weapons, which you can use to make unarmed strikes. If you hit with it, you deal slashing damage equal to 1d4 + your Strength modifier, instead of the bludgeoning damage normal for an unarmed strike. Short, sharp talons protrude from the ends of Tengu's hands, which can be dangerous if striking vital areas.

Genbu

  • Ability Scores: You have a +2 bonus to Wisdom and a +1 bonus to Intelligence. Genbu are highly valued in the Dragon Empire as advisors and teachers.
  • Size: You are a Medium creature. Genbu are very heavy, but not very tall, with most barely reaching a height of five feet when standing up straight (though Genbu usually stand slightly hunched over) yet weighing weighing between 250 and 300 pounds due to their dense shells.
  • Movement: You have a 25 foot land speed and a 30 foot swim speed. Their heavy shells slow down Genbu on land, but this is mitigated in the water.
  • Languages: You can speak, read, and write Common and Daitien.
  • Natural Armor: Your base AC is 17 (your Dexterity modifier doesn't affect this number). You gain no benefit from wearing armor, but if you are using a shield, you can apply the shield's bonus as normal. The thick shell of a Genbu makes it impossible to wear armor effectively, but it provides significant protection on its own.
  • Water Breathing: You can breathe air and water. Genbu are acclimated to both life on land and in the water.
  • Learned: You have proficiency in one skill of your choice. Genbu consider practical knowledge to be an important part of any well-rounded education.
  • Withdraw: You can withdraw into your shell as an action. until you emerge, you gain a +4 bonus to AC, and you have advantage on Strength and Constitution saving throws. While in your shell, you are prone, your speed is 0 and can't increase, you have disadvantage on Dexterity saving throws, you can't take reactions, and the only action you can take is a bonus action to emerge from your shell. Genbu can retreat into their durable shells for safety.

Tesso

  • Ability Scores: You have a +2 bonus to Dexterity and a +1 bonus to Intelligence. Tesso are quick on their feet and have a sharp wit.
  • Size: You are a Small creature. The average height of a Tesso is 4 feet tall, with a weight of 60 pounds.
  • Movement: You have a 30 foot land speed.
  • Languages: You can speak, read, and write Common and Daitien.
  • Darkvision: You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray. Tesso eyesight has adapted to the lighting conditions of back alleys and dark streets.
  • Burst of Speed: When you move on your turn in combat, you can double your speed until the end of the turn. Once you use this trait, you can't use it again until you move 0 feet on one of your turns. In an emergency, Tesso are able to push their limits to run at incredible speeds.
  • Naturally Stealthy: You can attempt to hide even when you are obscured only by a creature that is at least one size larger than you. Tesso have a knack for blending in with crowds and hiding behind larger friends.
  • Low Profile: You can move through the space of any creature that is of a size larger than yours. Tesso are used to moving through crowds of people larger than themselves.

Kitsune

  • Ability Scores: You have a +2 bonus to Charisma and a +1 bonus to Intelligence. Kitsune are naturally clever, and are adept at leveraging this wit in negotiations with others.
  • Size: You are a Medium creature. Most Kitsune are a little over five feet tall, and their weight is comparable to that of humans of similar height.
  • Movement: You have a 35 foot land speed. After their exile, Kitsune had to become accustomed to traveling quickly on foot.
  • Languages: You can speak, read, and write Common, Daitien, and one extra language of your choice.
  • Darkvision: You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray. Ancient kitsune were nocturnal and developed effective night vision.
  • Shrewd Negotiator: You have proficiency in the Persuasion and Insight skills. You have advantage on Persuasion and Insight rolls when making a deal or trading with somebody. Part of a Kitsune's natural talent for negotiation is not just in persuasiveness, but also their ability to read others.
