Kitsune

Fallen from divinity and exiled for unspeakable crimes, Kitsune are a race with a troubled past. Somehow Kitsune have brought themselves back into the civilized world, now embracing their mortality and working to develop new relationships with the inhabitants of Maerund.

Racial Traits

  • Ability Scores: You gain a +2 bonus to Charisma and a +1 bonus to Intelligence. Kitsune are naturally clever, and are adept at leveraging this wit in negotiations with others.
  • Size: You are a Medium creature. Most Kitsune are a little over five feet tall, and their weight is comparable to that of humans of similar height.
  • Movement: You have a 30 foot land speed.
  • Languages: You can speak, read, and write Common and Daitien.
  • Darkvision: You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray. Ancient kitsune were nocturnal and developed effective night vision.
  • Divine Ancestry: You have resistance to necrotic damage and radiant damage. Though they have been mortal for most of known history, Kitsune retain some hint of their divine nature.
  • Shrewd Negotiator: You gain proficiency in the Persuasion and Insight skills. Part of a Kitsune's natural talent for negotiation is not just in persuasiveness, but also their ability to read others.
  • Divine Grace: As an action, you can touch a creature and cause it to regain a number of hit points equal to your level. Once you use this trait, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest. The little divine power still present in Kitsune can be channeled as healing magic.

Example Names

  • Female Given Names: Adanna, Chinasa, Kiruka, Palesa, Retha

Physiology

Kitsune resemble Humans but have vulpine ears and tails. They also have much more prominent canine teeth than Humans do, which lends itself to a diet that favors proportionally more meat than vegetation. Kitsune eyes resemble those of foxes as well, and come in a striking variety of bright colors, which can be both beautiful and unnerving depending on who one asks. The physical builds of Kitsune are comparable to that of shorter Humans, with the tallest Kitsune reaching a height of about five and a half feet. Their divine origins grant Kitsune a fairly long lifespan of about 150 years on average, though they reach adulthood at about the same rate as Humans.

History

Kitsune were not originally mortal, but were demigods created by the goddess Amaterasu for her own amusement. When they were made mortal, Kitsune were driven mad when they realized life would not only bring them new joys, but also new suffering. Kitsune began to perform profane sacrifices to regain their immortality in a string of murders known as the Blood Scourge. The combined forces of the Dragon Empire would put an end to the experiments with force and banished the Kitsune people to the Scarred Wastes, a vast desert north of the Dragon Empire. The settlements established by Kitsune in the region would grow to become the Great Merchant Clans; these roving trader clans would work to bringing Kitsune out of isolation and earn the trust of the other races of Maerund.

Culture

Many Kitsune are travelers by nature, making their fortunes as traders and treasure hunters. Though this is not universal, a Kitsune settlement still needs farmers and tradespersons to create food and goods, free time is still often spent on travel, whether it is a trip to a nearby city or a quick rest away from home. Though some pockets of Sunsworn cultists still exist, most Kitsune harbor feelings of guilt and shame for their ancestors' role in the Blood Scourge and make efforts to cultivate positive relationships with other races. The horrific consequences of trying to fight their mortality has led Kitsune to appreciate the small joys in life, and to understand bad times are only temporary if one can fight through them.

Religion

Kitsune culture values both personal freedom and forgiveness, leading many Kitsune to worship Beldarin and Fereleos. Kitsune travelers often pray to Beldarin or even Di Jun that they will be safe and see exciting sights on their journey, while Fereleos is popular with those who wish to find redemption for the atrocities committed by the Sunsworn.
