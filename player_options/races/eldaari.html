Eldaari

Eldaari are long-lived, far-sighted, and most of all, extremely proud. Their race has outlived many others, and those that mock their meticulous planning and patience in all affairs simply have not seen as much as the Eldaari have.

Racial Traits

  • Ability Scores: You gain a +2 bonus to Intelligence and a +1 bonus to Dexterity. The Eldaari are known for their scholarly achievements, and their slender frames make them naturally agile.
  • Size: You are a Medium creature. Few Eldaari are shorter than six feet tall; the average Eldaari height is six feet four inches. They are also relatively thin, with an average weight of 140 pounds for an Eldaari of average height.
  • Movement: You have a 30 foot land speed.
  • Languages: You can speak, read, and write Common and Eldaarish.
  • Fundamental Magic: You know one cantrip of your choice from the Wizard spell list. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for it. All Eldaari, even if they do not study it further, are introduced to magic at a young age.
  • Arcane Insight: You can cast Detect Thoughts once per day. When you reach 3rd level, you can also cast Detect Magic once per day. When you reach 5th level, you can also cast See Invisbility spell once per day. You must finish a long rest to cast these spells once again with this trait. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for these spells. Eldaari perception is enhanced by their innate affinity for magic.
  • Arcane Poise: You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed, and magic can't put you to sleep. Generations of exposure to magic have made Eldaari minds less vulnerable to magical manipulation.

Example Names

  • Male Given Names: Hathol, Kelthar, Ophiel, Seredor, Teleroth
  • Female Given Names: Brennil, Cassiel, Eralen, Shoreli, Tishara
  • Surnames: Eilnesir, Felaben, Gwedphen, Gwilden, Talhien

Physiology

Eldaari are tall and slender, which combined with their long, pointed ears creates a very distinct silhouette, even from a distance. Their eyes are narrow and do not have visible pupils, which many other races can find unnerving. Eldaari blood is inherently imbued with magic, and this arcane influence has turned their skin deep purple and their eyes glowing amber. Furthermore, Eldaari have also developed extremely long lifespans, though at the cost of much lower birth rates. Physical maturation for an Eldaari is at around 20 years of age, but most Eldaari are not considered to be adults until they are at least 50 years old. They can live for upwards of 500 years if not killed by illness or injury, but most female Eldaari can give birth only once before becoming completely infertile. Lucky Eldaari are known to be able to have two or even three children, but some unlucky women are infertile to begin with.

Many Eldaari spellcasters can also begin to develop changes to their physical appearance based on the type of magic they focus on. Spellcasters that have inherent power or gain it through pacts such as sorcerers and warlocks this is typically based upon the origin of their power: sorcerers gifted by the Plane of Shadow, for example, may have their skin darken or their eyes turn completely black. Learned mages such as wizards will begin to reflect their chosen focus of study, with necromancers often becoming paler and more sickly in appearance. Such transformations are seen as a sign of individuality among Eldaari, though many other races often view it as a sign of increasing mutation in a people far too obsessed and reckless in their pursuit of mastery over magic.

History

The ancient Eldaari once occupied the Eldaari Empire, which spanned the entirety of the lands that now consitute both the Feldani Confederacy and all of the Western Kingdoms combined. The Eldaari Empire's expansion was fueled chiefly by arcane research which created advancements in both civil and military technologies. Cities glowed in the night with arcane lighting, magic engines propelled iron trains across the empire, and conquest was spearheaded by fire conjured from the heavens. The Eldaari Empire would not last, however, and would eventually collapse in the Feldani Revolution (referred to by many Eldaari scholars as the “Ungifted's Revolt”). The last of the Eldaari Empire's cities still held by its rulers now float off the western coast of Telaren, and make up the Eldaari Dominion.

Culture

Magic has always been important to the Eldaari, and those that possess the ability to harness it are held in high esteem. Though it is considered old-fashioned bigotry to look down on those who cannot use magic, there are some Eldaari mages who view non-mages as inferior beings. Due to their long lifespans and low birth rates, Eldaari value patience and caution in all affairs. Taking risks is not worth it when a single death could be the end of an entire family line. This also means a long-lived Eldaari warrior or adventurer can be assumed to either be incredibly careful, incredibly dangerous, or, as is more often the case, both.

Religion

Because of the importance of magic for Eldaari, many of them choose to worship Eiletynn over other deities. Ragnvaldr and Thaldar are also popular among those who value stability and order. Those who cannot use magic themselves usually worship deities related to their own professions or values, but many non-mage Eldaari choose to worship Eiletynn in spite of their lack of magical abilities.
