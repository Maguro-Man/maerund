Genbu

Their movements may be slow, but the mind of a Genbu is anything but. In the Dragon Empire Genbu are valued as philosophers, strategists, and advisors due to their inherent sagacity. This sort of wisdom would lead most Genbu to realize a life of adventure is neither stable nor safe, but such insight can't account for individual personality.

Racial Traits

  • Ability Scores: You gain a +2 bonus to Wisdom and a +1 bonus to Intelligence. Genbu are highly valued in the Dragon Empire as advisors and teachers.
  • Size: You are a Medium creature. Genbu are very heavy, but not very tall, with most barely reaching a height of five feet when standing up straight (though Genbu usually stand slightly hunched over) yet weighing weighing between 250 and 300 pounds due to their dense shells.
  • Movement: You have a 25 foot land speed and a 30 foot swim speed. Their heavy shells slow down Genbu on land, but this is mitigated in the water.
  • Languages: You can speak, read, and write Common and Daitien.
  • Natural Armor: Your base AC is 17 (your Dexterity modifier doesn't affect this number). You gain no benefit from wearing armor, but if you are using a shield, you can apply the shield's bonus as normal. The thick shell of a Genbu makes it impossible to wear armor effectively, but it provides significant protection on its own.
  • Water Breathing: You can breathe air and water. Genbu are acclimated to both life on land and in the water.
  • Learned: You gain proficiency in one skill of your choice. Genbu consider practical knowledge to be an important part of any well-rounded education.
  • Withdraw: You can withdraw into your shell as an action. until you emerge, you gain a +4 bonus to AC, and you have advantage on Strength and Constitution saving throws. While in your shell, you are prone, your speed is 0 and can't increase, you have disadvantage on Dexterity saving throws, you can't take reactions, and the only action you can take is a bonus action to emerge from your shell. Genbu can retreat into their durable shells for safety.

Example Names

Physiology

Genbu are bipedal, humanoid tortoises that stand upright, although many walk with a slight hunch to help support the weight of the thick shells that protect their backs. The leathery scales of Genbu can vary somewhat in color, with the most common colors being shades of brown, yellow, and green. Genbu shells have a similar range of color variance, but many Genbu choose to decorate their shells or paint them with vivid colors that may represent anything from one's ancestry to one's age and occupation. Genbu are somewhat stocky, but not as much as their weight would suggest; the majority of a Genbu's weight actually comes from their extremely dense and protective shells. It takes a relatively long time for Genbu to reach adulthood compared to most other races, about 25 years, and have an average lifespan of 200 years.

History

When the Dragon Empire was formed it was the Genbu that were chosen to make many decisions in its governance. Quickly gaining a reputation for fairness and wisdom in their suggestions, Genbu earned respect as the moderating presence behind the ruling Tatsu. However, the sage advice of prominent Genbu was not only used for civil services, and many Tengu generals kept Genbu retainers as military advisors. More ambitious Genbu chose not to offer their services as advisors at all, instead striking out on their own to become merchants or claim their own land. Many of these enterprising individuals would become founders of long-lasting merchant families or even see their land develop into fully-fledged cities through the generations.

Culture

Knowledge is extremely valuable to Genbu, not just its procurement, but also its application. Knowledge is not only useless if lost, but also if it not used in some way. Many Genbu look to history and the wisdom of their elders when making decisions so that they can learn lessons from past mistakes instead of repeating them. This does not mean that Genbu unconditionally adhere to the judgments of anyone older than them; learning is a constant process that continues until death for the Genbu and individuals of all ages are expected to collaborate and share their own views on issues. Even if Genbu generally consider those with more overall knowledge to be better equipped for life, unique perspectives and insight can be just more important when dealing with a relevant situation.

Religion

Di Jun is worshipped by most Genbu due to his role as the patron god of Genbu, though Thaldar and Eilettyn are popular as well. Genbu serving as a civil servants often look to Thaldar as representative of their duties, while those interested in more dangerous or arcane knowledge find a connection with Eilettyn.
